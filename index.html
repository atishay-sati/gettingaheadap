Our AP Courses

AP Physics C: Mechanics

Advanced Physics Concepts

Master classical mechanics including kinematics, dynamics, energy, momentum, and rotational motion. This comprehensive course covers the fundamental principles that govern the motion of objects, from simple particle motion to complex systems involving multiple forces and energy transformations. Students will develop strong problem-solving skills and gain a deep understanding of how the physical world operates.

  • • Kinematics & Dynamics - Motion analysis and force interactions
  • • Energy & Momentum - Conservation principles and transformations
  • • Rotational Motion - Angular kinematics and dynamics
  • • Gravitation & Oscillations - Universal forces and periodic motion

AP Physics C: Electricity & Magnetism

Advanced Physics Concepts

Dive into the essential concepts of AP Physics C: Electricity and Magnetism. This course builds a strong foundation in both theory and application, covering electric and magnetic phenomena in depth. Students will gain problem-solving strategies and a deeper understanding of how fields, forces, and energy interact.

  • • Electrostatics – Charge, electric fields, and electric potential
  • • Conductors, Capacitors, and Dielectrics – Electric fields in materials and energy storage
  • • Electric Circuits – Current, resistance, power, and circuit analysis
  • • Magnetic Fields and Electromagnetism – Magnetic forces, field sources, and right-hand rules
  • • Electromagnetic Induction – Induced EMF, Lenz’s law, and energy in inductors

AP Computer Science A

Programming & Algorithms

Learn Java programming, object-oriented design, and fundamental computer science concepts. This course provides a solid foundation in programming principles while developing computational thinking skills that are essential for success in computer science and related fields. Students will gain hands-on experience with real-world programming challenges and learn to think algorithmically.

  • • Java Programming - Syntax, control structures, and methods
  • • Object-Oriented Design - Classes, inheritance, and polymorphism
  • • Data Structures - Arrays, ArrayLists, and 2D arrays
  • • Algorithm Analysis - Efficiency and computational complexity

AP Chemistry

Chemical Principles

Explore atomic structure, molecular interactions, chemical reactions, and thermodynamics. This comprehensive chemistry course delves into the fundamental principles that govern matter and its transformations, providing students with a deep understanding of chemical processes and their applications in the natural world and technological innovations.

  • • Atomic Structure - Electron configurations and periodic trends
  • • Chemical Bonding - Ionic, covalent, and intermolecular forces
  • • Thermodynamics - Energy changes and spontaneity
  • • Kinetics & Equilibrium - Reaction rates and chemical balance

AP Language & Composition

Rhetorical Analysis

Develop critical reading and writing skills through rhetorical analysis and argumentation. This course emphasizes the power of language and communication, teaching students to analyze complex texts, construct compelling arguments, and express their ideas with clarity and sophistication. Students will master the art of persuasive writing and develop the analytical skills needed for college-level coursework.

  • • Rhetorical Analysis - Understanding author's purpose and strategies
  • • Argumentation - Constructing logical and persuasive arguments
  • • Synthesis Writing - Combining multiple sources effectively
  • • Research Methods - Evaluating sources and evidence

AP World History

Global Perspectives

Explore world history from ancient civilizations to modern global developments and interactions. This comprehensive survey course examines the major themes, events, and processes that have shaped human societies across time and space, helping students develop a nuanced understanding of historical causation and the interconnected nature of global developments.

  • • Learn to write the DBQ, LEQ, & SAQ and solve the MCQ for the AP exam
  • • Ancient Civilizations - Early human societies and cultural developments
  • • Global Trade Networks - Economic and cultural exchange systems
  • • Revolutions & Industrialization - Political and economic transformations
  • • Modern Global Issues - Contemporary challenges and international relations
