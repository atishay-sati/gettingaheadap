Electric Circuits

Electric Circuits is around 20%–30% of your AP test. This unit focuses on current, resistance, voltage, and power in electrical circuits. Understanding Ohm's law, Kirchhoff's rules, and circuit analysis helps solve problems involving resistors, capacitors, and complex circuit networks.

Now let's get into it!

Electric Circuits is the study of electrical current flow, resistance, voltage, and power in conductive pathways. In AP Physics C: Electricity & Magnetism, this unit introduces current, Ohm's law, Kirchhoff's rules, and circuit analysis techniques. It focuses on how electrical components like resistors and capacitors behave in series and parallel configurations, and how to analyze complex circuits using systematic methods. Mastery of electric circuits helps understand power distribution, energy transfer, and the behavior of electrical systems.
